so thinking of the frontend, what should our env contain?
- for now I think I should start with no log in, just whoever shows up is launched into a game, in the future we might make it so that people can log in and see stats about their old games.
- A guiding view on choosing what information env should contain is what information is relevant to the player, not per say the mechanics, as the mechanics will be handled by the server, and all that a player will be able to do is try a conquest with a specific set of pawns/power, pick a certain tribe, go into decline, etc but no mechanics needed per say
- there should be the set of available tribes, and how many coins are on each of those (though this feature could be put in later), tribes, tribes being a struct with race and trait
- There should be the current tribe of the player, as well as their in decline tribe.
- Potentially the server could hold the images associated with each tribe and power, and send the relevant ones according to the set of visible tribes.
- There should be whether it is their turn, as well as the amount of pieces/powers they have left to play (only relevant for display if it is the player's turn)
- There should be another component, potentially above the map, holding data about other users, such as whether it is their turn, if it is what pieces they have left to play in that case and what active and in decline tribe they have.
- the SVG should hold the information about the visual only, meaning it is the one calling the parser (I think long-term we would want the image of the map, the vertex file and potential scaling, to be transmitted by the server), and then it should use the environment for receiving what pawns are on what tiles etc. It is also the one that will receive clicks and as such should be the one trigger requests to the server. however, the playerinfo component will be the one holding the info about which tribe or power is selected, as well as potentially which phase of the game it is currently (conquest or redeployment), so while the "trigger" will be there, other important information should be stored in the environment.
- potential for having a "chat", where players could send messages as well as the server would log whatever is happening(this would reduce the need for animations which would be harder to implement)
